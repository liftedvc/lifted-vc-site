How It Works

The three steps to funding.

Good fundraising is built on best practices and knowing the industry's hidden rules. I make sure you walk in the room a seasoned pro — whether you're going for your first check or your Series A.

Step 01

Get Organized

Together, we build your dream investor target list, sharpen your narrative, and get your fundraising collateral and data room investor-ready — so when you're in front of the right people, everything lands, and your story feels inevitable.

Step 02

Be Pitch-Perfect

The goal is to demonstrate that you're venture-backable. We drill your pitch, sharpen investor communications, and build your warm intro pipeline through connector calls. You'll walk into meetings with confidence and credibility.

Step 03

Build Authentic FOMO

Real investor demand is engineered, not accidental. I work on timeline compression, parallel process management, coordinating conversations so multiple investors are in diligence at once, using soft commitments to pull others in, and knowing exactly when to communicate momentum.

Before tech, I spent five years on the USA Sailing Team, racing the 470 class internationally. That's where I learned to prepare obsessively and perform under pressure — habits that translated surprisingly well to fundraising.

My first tech role was as the first sales hire at inDinero, helping them hit $1M ARR (now valued at $1B+). That led me to launch Lifted.vc. Since then, I've coached 100+ founders from pre-seed through Series A, helping them raise over $80M in total capital.

FAQ

Good questions, straight answers.

From the founders who asked before you.

Do you take equity?
No. I don't take equity in your startup — I want to remain unbiased and solely focused on your fundraising goals, not on the long-term value of your company.
Do you really have a 70% success rate?
Yes. But context matters: I only work with founders I strongly believe can successfully fundraise. I care about my track record and only take on clients I'm confident I can help.
Who is the Fundraising Sprint for?
Founders raising their first or second non-friends-and-family round — typically pre-seed, seed, or Series A ($500K–$25M+). You should have a rough deck, some traction, a team, and a product.
How much time does the Sprint take?
Sprint meetings: 2–3 hrs/wk. Content: 1–2 hrs/wk. Async work (sourcing, networking, connector meetings): 20–60 hrs/wk. Fundraising is not passive — but I make sure that time goes toward the right things.
What if I'm not sure I'm ready to fundraise?
Ask me — readiness is complex. Market timing, revenue, product maturity, traction, and long-term funding outlook all factor in. I help you evaluate before you commit.
What's the selection process?
Fill out my 10-minute questionnaire. If it's a fit, I'll invite you to a 30-minute call. I review every application personally — this isn't a rubber-stamp process.
