About Me

I've been exactly
where you are.

I've founded and sold a YC company, raised from top-tier funds, and spent years on the investor side. I coach from lived experience, not theory.

Adam Roberts
Fundraising Coach · Lifted.vc

Before tech, I spent five years on the USA Sailing Team, racing the 470 class internationally. That's where I learned to prepare obsessively and perform under pressure, habits that translated surprisingly well to fundraising.

My first tech role was as the first sales hire at inDinero, helping them hit $1M ARR (now valued at $1B+). I then founded Paid Labs, went through Y Combinator S2014, and raised from Pear Ventures and Founder Collective before the company was acquired. After the sale, I joined Zinc Technologies (YC) as head of sales, then became an EIR at Wefunder, where I spent my days on the other side of the table and rediscovered what I love: helping founders turn a messy, terrifying process into something methodical and winnable.

That led me to launch Lifted.vc. Since then, I've coached 100+ founders from pre-seed through Series A, helping them raise over $80M in total capital.

My Coaching Philosophy

Coaching done differently.

I built Lifted around the coaching I wished I'd had when I was raising.

Founder-Focused
I've been in your shoes and understand your challenges. My advice comes from the founder perspective, not from an investor or a consultant who's never built.
Unbiased Sounding Board
Honest feedback with no ego or experience bias. I have no problem if you throw my advice in the mental trash bin, I just want you funded.
Tactical + Mental
I provide tactical strategies to get things done AND mental support to navigate the emotional rollercoaster of fundraising. Both matter equally.
Direct
You don't have time to waste. I cut to the chase, focus on what moves the needle, and help you get results, not give you homework you'll never do.
No Equity Taken
I don't take equity in your startup, I want to remain unbiased and solely focused on your fundraising goals.
Engaging by Design
Fundraising can suck, but I make it deliberately enjoyable and interactive. A burnt-out founder is not a funded founder.

FAQ

Good questions, straight answers.

From the founders who asked before you.

Do you take equity?
No. I don't take equity in your startup, I want to remain unbiased and solely focused on your fundraising goals, not on the long-term value of your company.
Do you really have a 70% success rate?
Yes. But context matters: I only work with founders I strongly believe can successfully fundraise. I care about my track record and only take on clients I'm confident I can help.
What if I'm not sure I'm ready to fundraise?
Ask me, readiness is complex. Market timing, revenue, product maturity, traction, and long-term funding outlook all factor in. I help you evaluate before you commit.
Can you guarantee success?
No, and be skeptical of anyone who does. Fundraising depends on factors no coach controls: market conditions, your business fundamentals, investor appetite. What I can promise is a process that gives you the best possible shot, and the honesty to tell you when something isn't working.
How much does it cost?
Most of my engagements run around $1k/month on retainer. That said, it's flexible. Sprints, ongoing coaching, and one-off working sessions are priced differently depending on what you need and how involved you want me to be. Reach out and we'll figure out what fits.
